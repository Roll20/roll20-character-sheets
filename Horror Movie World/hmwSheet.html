When you have leverage over another character, and you try to get something from them, roll with Charms. What counts as â€œleverageâ€ may differ, but usually you have to either exploit a personal relationship with this character or provide a definite argument why they should comply.

On a hit, you are able to convince, influence or manipulate them.

If it is a non-player character:

  • On a 10+, they do what you want.
  • On a 7-9, they do what you want, but with a troublesome condition or request.
  • On a 7-9, they do what you want, but with a troublesome condition or request.
  • On a miss, you have angered them or created further complications.

If it is a player character:

  • On 10+, they take a sympathy point and +1 forward if they do what you want.
  • On 7-9, they take a sympathy point if they do what you want.
  • On a miss, they take a sympathy point if they do the opposite of what you want.

Struggle with horror

When you have to act all calm and rational in scary situations, roll with Bravery. This move covers any dangerous situations where no other move can be applied. It also covers situations where you have to react to something scary by standing still, hiding, holding your breath, etc.

On a hit, you overcome your fear, do what you want and do not succumb to panic.

On 7-9, you succeed but fear takes its toll: take -1 forward.

On a miss, you are so scared you mess things up: take a fear point, and the Director makes a move against you.

Helping another character can also trigger this move. On a hit, the character you have helped takes +1 forward. You still resolve the move according to the instructions above.

Charge

When you use violence and physical prowess to solve problems, roll with Body. On a hit, you harm your opposition or put them where you want (e.g. push the monster downstairs or break the door open).

Violence always has consequences. On 10+, choose one. On 7-9, choose two.

  • You suffer harm
  • You end up in danger
  • You lose a sympathy point
  • You take a fear point
  • You suffer trauma (can only be chosen once per movie)

Think on the spot

When you come up with a solution or escape plan in the middle of danger, roll with Smarts. On a hit, you can ask questions about your predicament, and The Director has to answer honestly. On 10+, ask two. On 7-9, ask one.

  • How can I get out of here?
  • Where can I hide?
  • How can I hinder this threat?
  • How can I protect _______?

If you act according to answers, take +1 ongoing while the information is relevant. If, for example, you escape from the madmanâ€™s house, keep +1 ongoing until you are outside.

Get lucky

When you want something fortunate to happen to you, spend a sympathy point and choose one:

  • take +1 forward
  • find something useful
  • create an opportunity

If you want to take +1 forward, you need to spend a sympathy point before your next move.

If you choose to find something useful, you declare that you can easily get just the right item for your immediate problem. For example, you have a gun in your locket to stand a chance against zombies. The limit here is that an item only gives you a chance to succeed, not a guaranteed success.

If you choose to create an opportunity, you make a slight alteration to the scene. For example, when you are trapped in a windowless basement, you can say that the basement does have a window, just a small one. The limit here is that you can only alter one detail: you can say the basement has a window or that it is bulletproof - but not both. This alteration also cannot resolve the sceneâ€™s conflict entirely, only give you an advantage.

Come back

When your character dies or turns insane, you have a chance to bring them back. Spend 5 sympathy points and describe how your character survived. Maybe we did not see what really happened, or maybe the mortal wound was not so mortal after all, or maybe your character recovers after a short panic.

If you bring a dead character back to life, take 1 Health point back. Your character can appear in the next scene, any moment you want.

If you bring back a character who turned insane from fear, remove 1 fear point. Your character stays sane, and you can play them further.
