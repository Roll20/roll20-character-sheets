Quick Reference


HP Skills

Breathless


You can hold your breath for up to an hour, stacks with other similar bonuses. Additionally, you are Immune moves with the Powder Keyword.

Fasting


Fasting has to do with how long you can hold out for food and water. While most Trainers can go for four days without water and two weeks without food, you can withstand a week without water and a month without food. Your Pokmon take on this Disciplined behavior as well while part of your roster, requiring food and drink only as often as you. Additionally, your Pokemon dont ever dislike flavors and dont lose Loyalty from being fed Repulsive medicines.

Endurance


The Endurance Skill has to do with your Ability to withstand exhausting situations. You do not roll Tireless checks due to traversal or the effects of overworld weather, such as extreme heat/cold. Additionally, you dont take damage from sources other than damage die rolls and Negative Statuses. This doesnt prevent damage from falling or drowning.

Resistant


If you are resistant, you reduce the damage you would usually take from Poisoning and Burns by half your base HP stat rounded up. Additionally, when you roll to resist or cure a Negative Status on yourself you add +4 to the result.

Skill Checks

Attack Checks

Browbeat


When negotiations dont go well and you dont need to be nice, Browbeat is used for intimidation and muscling your way through problems. Whenever Browbeat is used, make a contested check versus a Trainers Concentration Skill or a Pokemons Power Skill. If you win this check the target is scared of you and is not willing to act against you during the encounter, potentially attempting to flee, while some Characters may only be impressed by your conviction. Either way you and your allies add +5 to Social Skill Checks made against characters you have successfully browbeaten.

Jump


The Jump skill allows for longer distance leaps and higher reach. A normal standing Jump for a Trainer is 0.6m and a standard leap is 1.5 m (no action required). Further Jumps and moving leaps require a proper check as a Trainer Action.

1 You trip and fall prone. (While prone you spend +1 shift to move, cant fly and need to stand up by spending half your Shift, rounded up.)
5 You jump a normal height 0.6m, or leap forward a normal distance of 1.5m
10 You jump higher, 1.2m, or leap forward 3m
15 You leap upwards 2.4m, or leap forward 6m
20 Adrenaline pumping, you leap upwards 3m, or leap forward 9m
30 Adrenaline pumping, you leap upwards 6m, or leap forward 18m

Sprint


Whenever you need to get somewhere fast, Sprint. As a Trainer Action you can attempt to Sprint, getting farther than your normal shift in a round.

1 You trip and fall prone. (While prone you spend +1 shift to move, cant fly and need to stand up by spending half your Shift, rounded up.)
5 You make some progress, Shifting again with half your Shift speed rounded up.
10 You Shift again with your full Shift speed.
15 Youre getting out of there, Shifting again with +3 to your Shift speed.
20 Youre getting out of there, Shifting again with +6 to your Shift speed.
30 Youre a Speed Master! Shift again with double your Shift speed then at the end of your turn, Shift again with double your Shift speed.

Strength


Regardless of your Attack Stat, proper form is needed to really use the muscle you may build. Strength will help you properly use your Attack to lift or move heavy objects. As a Trainer Action you can attempt to increase your lifting, pushing or pulling force. The Attack increase from Strength doesnt apply to your Attack modifier and disappears at the end of your next turn. If you make multiple Strength checks you only benefit from the highest checks DC you have met.

1 You tense up and strain yourself, losing 5 Attack, decreasing force by 20 kg.
5 You use your Strength as normal.
10 You add 5 to your Attack, increasing force by 20 kg.
15 You dig in adding 10 to your Attack, increasing force by 40 kg.
20 Through tremendous effort you add 15 to your Attack, increasing force by 60 kg.
30 Adrenaline pumping, you add 30 to your Attack, increasing force by 120 kg.
   Grapple: You may use Strength to attempt a Contested Grapple Check versus an adjacent Character. For Trainers they use Strength/Acrobatics Skill and for Pokemon they use their Power Skill, you win ties for this check. If you win the Contested check you Grapple the Target imposing the Grappled Condition on them as the Grappler. You may only Grapple one Character per hand you have free.

Defense Checks

Concentration


Whenever you take damage performing a delicate task in battle or require focus to complete a task you may need to make a Concentration check. Some features require Concentration checks, if they have a set value you must refer to those. Otherwise when making Concentration checks, you need to roll at least 10 or the damage taken divided by 4, rounded down to maintain your focus, whichever is higher.

Deflection


During your turn you can make a Deflection check as a Trainer Action to ward against attackers by taking a defensive stance. Once you make this check you benefit from its effect(s) until the start of your next turn. The Defense and Special Defense modifier increases from Deflection apply for evasion but not for any other calculation, check, save or feature. The stat increases apply to your defenses only when calculating damage taken. If you make multiple Deflection checks you only benefit from the highest checks DC you have met.

1 You gain no defenses.
5 You add +2 to your Defense and Special Defense.
10 You add +4 to your Defense and Special Defense.
15 You add +6 to your Defense and Special Defense.
20 You add +10 to your Defense and Special Defense.
30 You add +20 to your Defense and Special Defense.

Healing


Healing checks are typically done at a Camp Rest where you recoup from damage taken. During encounters certain items or Skills may allow for the use of a Healing check. Whoever is the target of your Healing action recovers the HP described by the Tier you meet and for percentage healing, always round up.

1 You recover no HP.
5 You recover a normal 10% of your maximum HP.
10 You recover 15% of your maximum HP.
15 You recover 25% of your maximum HP.
20 You recover 50% of your maximum HP and recover from a Negative Status.
30 You recover all HP, remove all Negative Status and gain 10% of your maximum HP as Temporary HP.

Tireless


Your journey can be difficult and taxing especially on your physical body. If you push your limits too far, either from traveling a long distance without break, forging food and water for too long or even forgoing sleep for more than 24 hours your GM may ask for a Tireless check to see how much of a toll your adventure is taking on your Trainer. HP loss and unconsciousness from Tireless cannot be healed outside of Center visits and certain Powerful Pokemons Skills. You do not reduce maximum HP from Tireless HP reductions, you instead just cannot heal that amount of HP below your maximum.

1 Medical Emergency! You fall unconscious immediately dropping to 0 HP. You must have medical attention at a Center to wake up.
5 Its getting really bad, you lose 50% of your max HP.
10 Dizziness, you lose 25% of your max HP.
15 You feel tired, you lose 15% of your max HP.
20 You feel fine, losing no HP for now.
30 Somehow, you feel better than before, you gain 10% of your max HP as Temporary Hit points and lose no HP for now.

Special Attack Checks

Engineering


With engineering you can understand and operate machinery be it mundane or futuristic. Certain machines may still stump you, requiring an engineering check to identify how to repair or operate them. In these cases your GM will ask for a check to see if you can do so.

1 The device is Foreign to you, you cannot identify it further.
5 You can guess its purpose but anything further including how it is operated is unknown to you.
10 You know the item's purpose and can successfully turn it off and on. Operations are still beyond you.
15 You can repair, operate and use the device.
20 You are an expert in the device and can use it without issue.
30 You can replicate the device and its functionality given resources and time. Additionally, you have knowledge of how and why it was made.
   Survival: You can make an Engineering check to determine natural phenomena such as foraging for food or determining the location of a good campsite. You may also use this check to roughly track Wilds and Trainers that have been in an area recently. You will not know what DC you need to make but, you should aim to roll as high as possible.

History


When it comes to the past of the world (or journey) History is where youll find it. When you learn of a place or significant event you may make a History check to see how much you know about it. Your GM may ask for History checks when you are unsure about an event happening in the past if you can reasonably know of it.

1 You dont know anything about it.
5 Youve heard of the place/event in passing, you werent paying attention.
10 10 You know about the place - any Gyms, major sights, famous people from there You know about the event - who/what/when/why it happened.
15 You know about the Gyms specialties, team specifics of the famous people from there You know about details that are often left out in history books.
20 Itd be hard to tell that you hadnt been to the place before, or werent at the event personally.
30 You may as well have written the primary source on the topic. Your expertise in the subject may even eclipse what is in History books. You may even have information on how and why the event took place.

Investigate


When trying to find somethings purpose or locate a hidden item, Investigation can be used to find them. Your GM may ask for an Investigation check when you want to find something hidden, in this case they determine the DC of the check, rolling higher is always better.
   Detect Residue: You can detect Psychic residue from those affected mentally by Psionics through careful questioning and observations. Different Psychic residues have a hue and a weight that can be sensed by Aura or by Psychics with their gifts and are identical across several minds it has touched, and once you meet its source its recognizable. You will not know the feature that applied the effect unless you roll high enough.

Programming


When confronted with a network of computers or when working undercover Programming allows for interaction in the digital world. When using these networks your GM may ask for a Programming check contested by the Network itself or a Trainers Programing or Pokemons Intelligence within the network.
   Supernatural: In settings without computers or with your GMs approval Programming checks may be used for the supernatural. When performing supernatural feats your GM may ask for a Programming check which may be contested by another Character. If the check is not contested you will not know what DC you need to make but, you should aim to roll as high as possible.

Special Defense Checks

Bluff/Diplomacy


Diplomacy is often more efficient than battle and sometimes deception is needed for even honest heroes. When a key moment in a conversation has the possibility of swaying a Trainer or Pokemon your GM will ask for a Bluff/Diplomacy check, you will not know what DC you need to make but, you should aim to roll as high as possible.

Perception


When in the moment some things may slip you by, Perception stops that from happening. You may be asked to roll perception by the GM* when you need to contest Stealthing Trainers/ Invisible Pokemon or when an event is occurring out of your immediate view. Your GM will not tell you the DC for these checks or even why you are making them, but you want to roll high.
   Passive Perception: 1/Round your GM may ask you to make a contested Perception check as a Free Action versus any Invisible Character(s). Do not act as if you noticed anything if you fail this check as your character didnt.

*Note for Group Perception: Elect one Trainer/Pokemon Player to make a Perception check for the group outside of action use in battle.

Sooth


Sometimes just words will not calm down wild Pokemon. Sooth checks are made only versus Pokemon that are agitated. If your action would reasonably Sooth the Pokemon you roll this check.

1 The Wild Pokemon is further agitated and attacks you immediately (regardless of turn order, no action used).
5 The Wild Pokemon is uncertain if you have good intent at all.
10 The Wild Pokemon is no longer hostile to you, it is still weary.
15 The Wild Pokemon does not see you as a threat anymore, if agitated it is now Neutral. If you fail more than one capture check versus them, they will flee.
20 The Wild Pokemon sees your as trustworthy, if you fail 3 capture checks versus them, they will flee.
30 The Wild Pokemon now considers you a friend and is loyal to you, You do not roll to capture this Pokemon, it automatically is captured and has the effect of the Friend Ball (in addition to whatever the capture device does).

Streetwise


Sometimes NPCs are not to be trusted, Streetwise will help you discern their evil plans. If you wish to discern if a Character is being truthful with you, you can make a contested Streetwise check versus a Trainers Bluff/Diplomacy or a Pokemons Intelligence. Alternatively, if you are attempting to find your way or figure out where important places are you can roll Streetwise, you will not know the DC but you must roll high.

Speed Checks

Acrobatics


With style and grace you traverse obstacles in your path. Whenever there is a question on keeping your footing or even if you are flexible enough to go over a fence, acrobatics checks can be made (no action required).

1 You fall Prone. Your shift ends and your shift skills are halved during the turn you get up.
5 You can traverse a normal fence or walk across a board wide enough to normally walk.
10 You can balance on the edge of a fence, or scale a story of a building.
15 You can traverse thin paths without losing speed and can scale the side of a building.
20 You have no trouble scaling buildings or overcoming any pathways.
30 No feat of Dexterity can stop you, any flourish you wish to add is done gracefully. You can scale a skyscraper.

Perform


Performance may be used to distract, entertain or even earn poke. If you make multiple Performance checks you only benefit from the first poke you earn or lose for that day.

1 You fail and are making a fool of yourself losing 1d6x10 poke.
5 You gain no attention from anyone.
10 You are able to gain the passing attention of passersby gaining 1d4x10 poke.
15 You gain the attention of those without anything going on gaining 1d6x10 poke.
20 You are the center of attention and gain 2d6x10 poke.
30 You have a once in a lifetime performance gaining all attention of everyone around and earning 3d20x10 poke.

Sleight of Hand


While directly stealing is bad, sleight of hand can be used for tricks and concealing items. Whenever Sleight of Hand is called for make a Contested check versus a Trainers Perception Skill or a Pokemons Intelligence Skill.

Stealth


Stealth allows for you to get the jump on wilds and hide yourself better. Unlike other skills you declare your intent to Stealth, gaining the Hidden Condition from the Characters you are hiding from. If you use a Weapon Attack, use a Trainer feature on another Trainer/Pokemon, or are in the open your stealth ends. Additionally, each time a Trainer rolls Perception or a Pokemon rolls Intelligence to find you, you must contest with a Stealth check versus them, you win ties.

Status Afflictions

Negative Status Afflictions

Asleep


On the first round of Sleep, a 16 or better will wake you. On following turns, you must roll two less than the previous turn to wake up, capping at 6 (16,14,12,10,8,6). On a 10 or higher the Pokemon may Shift but cannot use a Move. On a failed roll, the Pokmon may not use a Move or Shift. When a Pokmon uses Rest, it remains asleep to two turns no matter what, no longer no shorter. When a Sleeping Pokmon is sent out of its PokeBall, the Sleep check starts at 12. On Turns following that, you must roll two lower, capping at 6.

Burned


While Burned, the Attack Stat is halved, rounded down. Once during your turn, you may try to roll for self curing the Burn in place of a Trainer Action or Move. On d20, the check is a 16 or higher. Fire-Type Pokmon cannot be burned. At the end of every round, a Burned Pokmon loses 1/10th of its Max HP. A Trainer instead loses 1/20th of his or her Max HP. A Pokmon does not suffer the effects of Burn while in a Poke Ball or while at rest out of combat or in a Pokeball. A Trainer does not suffer the effects of Burn while at rest.

Confused


Before using a Move or Shifting Pokmon roll 1d20. On 1-6, you deal twice your STAB to yourself as a Move action. Do not apply Weakness, Resistance, Defense or Special Defense. On 7-15, you may use a Move and Shift as normal. On 16 or higher you are cured of confusion. Trainers instead Must roll 1d20, they are unable to act on 1-6, may act as normal on 7 or higher and are cured once at rest. When a Character exits the encounter, they are cured of Confusion.

Flinched


You may not Act, Shift or use a Move during your next turn, then Flinch is cured. A Trainer that is Flinched may choose to use an Action or Shift but not both, then Flinch is cured.

Frozen


When Frozen you may not Act, use a Move without the Thaw Keyword or Shift. At the start of your turn you can choose to expend your Move Action or Trainer Action to attempt to break free of Frozen. On a d20 check the DC is 16. On a 16 or higher you are Defrosted and have all of your actions. Ice Type Pokmon cannot be Frozen. If you are hit by a move that breaks walls or use a move that has the Thaw Keyword, you are Defrosted. A Pokemon with Flash Fire active is not Frozen but loses any bonus from Flash Fire.

Infatuation


Before using a Move or Shifting a Pokemon rolls 1d20. On 1-10, you may not target the offender you are Infatuated towards with a Move. On 11-19 you may use a Move and Shift as normal.
For Trainers roll 1d20, on 1-6 you may not command your Pokemon to target the offender you are infatuated towards with Moves nor may you use Harmful Actions versus them. On 7-10 you can command as normal but cannot use Harmful Actions versus the offender you are infatuated towards. On 11-19 you can act as normal and command as normal.
On a 20 or higher, you are cured of the Infatuation as any Character. If the offender you are infatuated towards is KOd or leaves the field the infatuation ends.

Paralysis


While Paralyzed, the Speed Stat is halved, rounded down. On the first round of Paralysis, roll 6 or better on 1d20 to act as usual. On a failed roll you cannot use a Move or Shift. Electric Type Pokmon cannot be paralyzed. Instead of a Move or Trainer Action on your turn you can attempt to self cure Paralysis. On a d20, the check is a 16 or higher. After 1 hour of rest, Paralysis is cured.

Poisoned


While Poisoned, the Special Attack Stat is halved, rounded down. Once during your turn, you may try to roll for self-curing the Poison in place of a Pokmons Move or Trainer Action. You may not try this while Badly Poisoned. On d20, the check is a 16 or higher. Poison and Steel type Pokmon are immune to becoming Poisoned and Badly Poisoned. At the end of every round, the Poisoned loses 1/10th of its total HP. A Pokmon does not suffer the effects of Poison while in a Poke Ball or while at rest out of combat. A Trainer instead loses 1/20th of his or her Max HP while Poisoned. A Trainer does not suffer the effects of any kind of Poison while at rest.

Badly Poisoned


While Badly Poisoned, the afflicted loses 5 HP, then loses double that amount next round (to 10 HP, 20 HP, 40 HP etc) at the end of each round. While at 0 hit points Badly Poisoned forces Death checks to be at disadvantage. Damage from Badly Poisoned fails Death checks throws only if lethal intent was used on the feature or move that afflicted the status. A Trainer must seek medical attention if Badly Poisoned. A Trainer does not suffer the effects of any kind of Poison while at rest.

Positive Status Afflictions

Critical Hit


When a Character rolls a Critical Hit they gain this status and their attack gains Never-Miss. The user of an attack that lands a Critical Hit adds the Damage Dice Roll a second time against the target, including any base amount in the Damage Dice Roll. The move ignores all positive combat stages on the target and all negative combat stages on the user. Critical Hit is lost after dealing the damage of the attack.

Dynamax-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would be affected by an attack or take the move action on its turn it Dynamaxes for the next 3 Rounds before resolving the move. This Status is then lost.

Mega-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would be affected by an attack or take the move action on the initiative of its mega statline it Mega Evolves for the next 10 minutes before resolving the move. This Status is then lost.

Tera-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would be affected by an attack or take the move action it Terastalizes for the next 10 minutes before resolving the move. This Status is then lost.

Z-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would take the move action during its turn it selects its Z-move according to its held Z-Crystal. This Status is then lost.

Conditions


Ally/Helpful


This denotes a Character that is currently aiding you in battle. Helpful Pokemon are typically Helpful for a short duration and take your commands. Your GM may simply state if a Character is an Ally/Helpful or Enemy/Foe/Hostile and not mark them, if you need clarification ask them.

Enemy/Foe/Hostile


This Character is your opponent in battle, roleplay or otherwise. Some effects apply to these Characters. Your GM may simply state if a Character is an Ally/Helpful or Enemy/Foe/Hostile and not mark them, if you need clarification ask them.

Fainted


This condition marks a Character that has been defeated by losing all of their HP. This Character has the following effects:
- They are Prone (and Unconscious if the attack was with lethal intent).
- This Character cant use Move Actions, Swift Actions, Free Actions or Shift Actions.
- Any Healing received will still restore HP but will not remove Fainted.
- Do not remove Fainted at the end of an encounter. Remove this Condition after a Center Rest or 1d4+1 hours pass. Revives and other features can remove Fainted.

Grappled


This condition marks a grouping of characters one being the Grappler and the other being the Grappled. The Grappled Character has the following effects:
- This Character and the Grappler cannot Shift.
- If this Character is moved the Grappler is moved the same amount and direction.
- This Character may attempt a Contested Strength (Power for Pokemon) check if the Grapple doesnt have a set turn duration, on a success the Condition ends, the Grappler wins ties.
- If this Character Teleports or becomes Intangible the Condition ends.

Hidden


This Condition denotes a Character that is unseen by their foes either by Stealth or other means. A Hidden Character has the following effects:
- You are not seen by foes.
- You roll twice on attack rolls against Characters that cant see you taking the higher result.
- You are not a target for features.
- Your Shift Skills are halved and you cannot Sprint.
- If you and all allies are hidden and begin an encounter with foes you each take a turn before the normal initiative, you still act on your first turn in initiative as normal.
- If you attack or are spotted by a foe you lose this Condition.

Intangible


An Intangible Character has the following benefits:
- You ignore the effects of Difficult Terrain.
- You can pass through Blocking Terrain, Objects and Characters.
- If you end your turn while in Blocking Terrain, a solid Object or another Character you lose 1d12 HP.
- If you lose this condition and are inside Blocking Terrain, a solid Object or another Character you lose of your maximum HP and are shifted onto the nearest unoccupied space.
- If you use a Trainer/Move Action or make an attack this Condition ends.

Invisible


You have the following effects while Invisible:
- You cant be seen by other Characters using normal vision.
- Attacks made against you roll twice and use the lower result.
- If you use an attack you lose this condition.

KOd


This Condition applies to a Character who is currently Fainting and allows them to resolve any Free Action Triggers at that time. Some Triggers care about this Condition such as the Aftermath, Beast Boost or Moxie Abilities.

Prone


This Character has collapsed onto whatever surface is under them. While Prone a Character has the following effects:
- This Character must spend an additional +1 Shift to move from one space to another.
- This Character cannot benefit from Sky Shift.
- A Character may spend half their Shift, rounded to stand up ending this Condition.

Trapped


Ghost-Type Characters cant be Trapped.While Trapped a Character has the following effects:
- This Character cant make Escape checks nor can they be recalled to a Pokeball.
- This Characters Shift Skills are halved and you cannot Sprint.
- This Character cannot be Shifted from the effects of Keywords other than Push.
- If this Character uses a Move with the Dash or Leap Keyword not during their turn in initiative they may spend their Shift Action for the round to Shift during that move instead.
- The Trapped Condition is removed if the offender who applied the Condition is KOd or leaves the encounter.

Semi-Invulnerable


A Character with the Semi-Invulnerable condition causes Moves and Weapon Attacks to automatically miss them. If a move gains Never-Miss specifically to hit a Semi-Invulnerable target the move cannot be avoided with this status and automatically hits this Character. Semi-Invulnerable ends at the end of the Characters next turn.

Unconscious


This Character has the following Effects:
- This Character follows the Dying rules (if applicable to the campaign).
- This Character cannot be restored with Revives or similar items and must be recalled by an adjacent Character if theyre a Pokmon.
- Remove Unconscious only after a Center Rest.

This shows how much damage to multiply after defenses are calculated based on the defender's type and the type of the damaging attacking move.

Contest Quick Reference


Contest Keywords

Adagio

During the Performance, if the move does not target Self, decrease the AC of the move by 1, but decrease the Appeal roll by 2. During the Battle, if the user is Rhythmically Moving (The user shifted less than or equal to half their full Shift speed rounded down during the previous round), add 2d4 to the Appeal Roll. If a move gains the Dance Move Keyword, they also gain either Adagio or Allegro and lower the Appeal Dice by 1d4 to a minimum of 0d4.

All Out!

For the remainder of the encounter, lower the Appeal Roll dice of the user by 3d4. This only lowers the amount of dice rolled in appeal, do not subtract it by that amount. This keyword is applied even if this moves Appeal is not selected.

Allegro

During the Performance, if the move does not target self, increase the AC of the move by 1, but increase the Appeal roll by 1d4+2. During the Battle, if the user is Rhythmically Moving (The user shifted less than or equal to half their full Shift speed rounded down during the previous round), increase the Voltage of a judge by 1. If a move gains the Dance Move Keyword, they also gain either Adagio or Allegro and lower the Appeal Dice by 1d4 to a minimum of 0d4.

Critical Performance

If the user successfully lands a Critical Hit add 3d4 to the Appeal Roll instead of 2d4 from the Critical Hit Status. Moves that have or gain an increased chance to land a Critical hit gain Critical Performance while they have the boosted Critical Hit rate.

Destructive

If the move would be used to remove a Preparation Move token, add 2d4 to the Appeal Roll.

Excitement

If Excitement was not used in the previous round, next round all performers roll an additional 1d4 (Max. 1d4) to add to all Appeal Rolls. Excitement can only apply once during the Performance.

Fortify

Fortify moves block damage and or other effects, but they do not fully stop the Appeal Roll. In battle, If a move with Fortify would make a move miss, be unaffected or reduce damage, lower the Appeal Roll of the affected move by 2d4 and increase the users next Appeal Roll by 2d4. During the Performance If a move with Fortify would make a move miss, be unaffected or reduce damage, the users next Appeal Roll by 2d4. This keyword is applied even if this moves Appeal is not selected.

Get Ready

If the user uses a move that doesnt have "Get Ready" next round, Add 4d4 to the Appeal Roll.

Good Show

Choose one of the judges and raise their Voltage by 1.

Grand Finale

Grand Finale moves are very potent, but come at a great cost. During the performance, this move can only be used during the final round. If the move is used during a Battle and the Opponents Contest Points are reduced to 0, the user wins.

Helpful

Helpful are moves frequently used on allied Pokemon to help them out. During Battle, if this move targets an ally, lower the opponents Contest Points from this move's Appeal instead of the Targets Contest Points.

Hold That Thought

If the user missed a move last round that does not have Hold That Thought, roll the Appeal Roll that would have been rolled last round and add 3d4 to the Appeal Roll.

Inversed Appeal

If the user has missed an attack during the Performance Round, the user adds 5d4 to the Appeal Roll. During the Battle Round, the user adds the following to the Appeal Roll based on the percentage of remaining Contest Points.
% Contest Points     Additional Appeal
100%-50%                1d4
49%-20%                  2d4
19%-10%                  4d4
9%-1%                      8d4

Launch

Launch Moves push the target. During the Performance or battle, if a target would collide with Blocking Terrain or another character, add 1d4 to the Appeal. If in battle and a target would be pushed off the stage horizontally from the Push Move Keyword, the target is instead at the edge of the stage and adds 1d4 to the Appeal Roll.If a move gains the Move Keyword Push, the move also gains Launch.

Multi-Strike

For each successful hit in the Multi-Hit, add this Moves Appeal Dice to the Appeal Roll. If at least 1 attack was successful, roll Appeal. A move that has the Move Keyword Multi-Hit, gains Multi-Strike.

Preparation

Roll Appeal and additionally deal damage in the contest battle round. Then, the user can place 1 token that represents the attack within the attacks target range. If it is a blast/burst/column move, it occupies all legal space of the move. This token lasts until the end of the next round. Next Round, if a move is used that targets the token and successfully rolls its AC, the token is removed and adds 2d4 to the Appeal Roll. During the Battle, the token cannot be destroyed until the round after it is created. When in a Battle, always lower the Opponents Contest Points from the Appeal Roll regardless of who made the Preparation Token. A performer cannot make another Preparation Token while that same performer already made a Preparation Token that is on the field, even if the Preparation attack would destroy the token. This keyword is applied even if this moves Appeal is not selected.

Quick Set

The user can use their Move/Trainer Action first during initiative during the Battle next round. If more than one Pokemon is benefiting from Quick set apply the users speed. Only the users Move Action can be used first.

Resonance

Add Xd4 to the Appeal Roll where X is every different Resonance move the user previously used during the performance or battle. If a move gains the Sound Move Keyword, it gains Resonance. Then lower the Appeal Roll dice by 1d4 to a minimum of 0d4.

Reliable

Add Xd4 to the Appeal Roll where X is how many times the user successfully hit that move during the battle or performance.

Risky

These moves have high risk, but high reward. During a Performance Round, increase the AC of these moves by +2. During the battle, if the move damages the user, the user loses 1d4 of their Total Contest Points for every 10 points of damage the user takes from the move.

Round Starter

If it is the first round of the Battle or Performance, add 3d4 to the Appeal Roll.

Scrambler

Roll 1d10 then add the following to the Appeal Roll:
Result On d10     Additional Appeal
1                           1
2-4                        1d4
5-6                        2d4
7-8                        3d4
9                           4d4
10                         5d4

Seen Nothing Yet!

If the user rolled 5 or more Appeal last round, add 3d4 to the Appeal Roll. If you add the bonus 3d4, you cannot benefit from Seen Nothing Yet! during the rest of the Encounter.

Unsettling

During the Battle Round, this move cannot raise Voltage. Choose one of the judges and lower their Voltage by 1. During the Performance Round, if you lower a Judges Voltage add 2d4 to the Appeal Roll.




Contest Status Afflictions

Negative Status Afflictions

Asleep


On the first round of Sleep, a 11 or better will wake you. On following turns, you must roll four less than the previous turn to wake up, capping at 4 (11,7,3). On a 5 or higher the Pokemon may Shift but cannot use a Move. On a failed roll, the Pokmon may not use a Move or Shift. When a Pokmon uses Rest, it remains asleep to two turns no matter what, no longer no shorter. When a Sleeping Pokmon is sent out of its PokeBall, the Sleep check starts at 11. On Turns following that, you must roll two lower, capping at 3.

Burned


While Burned, the Attack Stat is halved, rounded down. Once per turn, you may try to roll for self curing the Burn in place of a Trainer Action or Move. On d20, the check is a 11 or higher. Fire-Type Pokmon cannot be burned. At the end of every round, a Burned Pokmon loses 1/20th of its Max HP. A Trainer instead loses 1/20th of his or her Max HP. A Pokmon does not suffer the effects of Burn while in a Poke Ball or while at rest out of combat. A Trainer does not suffer the effects of Burn while at rest or in a Pokeball.

Confused


Before using a Move or Shifting Pokmon roll 1d20. On 1-5, you deal your STAB to yourself as a Move action. Do not apply Weakness, Resistance, Defense or Special Defense. On 6-10, you may use a Move and Shift as normal. On 11 or higher, you are cured of Confusion. Trainers instead Must roll 1d20, they are unable to act on 1-5, may act as normal on 6-10, on 11 or higher the trainer is cured once at rest. When a Character exits the encounter, they are cured of Confusion.

Flinch


You may not Act, Shift or use a Move during your next turn, then Flinch is cured. A Trainer that is Flinched may choose to use an Action or Shift but not both, then Flinch is cured.

Frozen


When Frozen you may not Act, use a Move without the Thaw Keyword or Shift. At the start of your turn you can choose to expend your move action or Trainer Action to attempt to break free of Frozen. On a d20 check the DC is 11. On a 11 or higher you are thawed and have all of your actions. Ice Type Pokmon cannot be Frozen. If you are hit by a move that breaks walls or has the Thaw Keyword, you are Defrosted. A Pokemon with Flash Fire active is not Frozen but loses any bonus from Flash Fire.

Infatuation


Before using a Move or Shifting a Pokemon rolls 1d20. On 1-10, you may not target the offender you are Infatuated towards with a Move. On 11-19 you may use a Move and Shift as normal. When a Character exits the encounter they are cured of Infatuation.
For Trainers roll 1d20, on 1-6 you may not command your Pokemon to target the offender you are infatuated towards with Moves nor may you use Harmful Actions versus them. On 7-10 you can command as normal but cannot use Harmful Actions versus the offender you are infatuated towards. On 11-19 you can act as normal and command as normal.
On a 20 or higher, the afflicted is cured of Infatuation. If the offender the afflicted is infatuated towards becomes KOd or leaves the field the infatuation ends.

Paralysis


While Paralyzed, the Speed Stat is halved, rounded down. While Paralyzed, roll 5 or better on 1d20 to act as usual. On a failed roll you cannot move or shift. Electric Type Pokmon cannot be paralyzed. Instead of a Move on your turn you can attempt to self cure Paralysis. On a d20, if the result is 10 or higher, the user is cured. After 1 hour of rest, Paralysis is cured.

Poisoned


While Poisoned, the Special Attack Stat is halved, rounded down. Once per turn, you may try to roll for self-curing the Poison in place of a Pokmons Move. You may not try this while Badly Poisoned. On a d20, if the result is 11 or higher, the user is cured. Poison and Steel type Pokmon are immune to becoming Poisoned and Badly Poisoned. At the end of every round, the Poisoned loses 1/20th of its total HP. A Pokmon does not suffer the effects of Poison while in a Poke Ball or while at rest out of combat. A Trainer instead loses 1/20th of his or her Max HP while Poisoned. A Trainer does not suffer the effects of any kind of Poison while at rest.

Badly Poisoned


While Badly poisoned, the afflicted loses 3 HP, then loses double that amount next round (to 3 HP, 6 HP, 12 HP etc) at the end of each round. While at 0 hit points Badly Poisoned forces death saving throws to be at disadvantage. Damage from Badly Poisoned fails death saving throws only if lethal intent was used on the feature or move that afflicted the status. A Trainer does not suffer the effects of any kind of Poison while at rest.

Positive Status Afflictions

Critical Hit


When a Character rolls a Critical Hit they gain this status and their attack gains Never-Miss. The user of an attack that lands a Critical Hit adds the Damage Dice Roll a second time against the target, including any base amount in the Damage Dice Roll. The move ignores all positive combat stages on the target and all negative combat stages on the user. Critical Hit is lost after dealing the damage of the attack. The user adds 2d4 to the Appeal Roll as well as ignoring any effect that is lowering their Appeal Roll.

Dynamax-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would be affected by an attack or take the move action on its turn it Dynamaxes for the next 3 Rounds before resolving the move. While Dynamaxed, all d4s in the Appeal Roll become d6s. This Status is then lost.

Mega-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would be affected by an attack or take the move action on the initiative of its mega statline it Mega Evolves for the next 10 minutes before resolving the move. A Mega Evolved Pokemon adds 1d4 to all of their total Appeal Rolls. This Status is then lost.

Tera-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would be affected by an attack or take the move action it Terastalizes for the next 10 minutes before resolving the move. When using a move where damage is boosted from Terastalization, add 2d4 to the total Appeal Roll. This Status is then lost.

Z-Surge


The first time this Pokmon would take the move action during its turn it selects its Z-move according to its held Z-Crystal. If the move becomes a boosted move from Z-Surge (typically from non-damaging moves), add 5d4 to the Appeal Roll. If the move would become a different move being Boosted from Z-Surge, replace the Moves Contest Keywords and effects with the Z-Moves effect. If the Move adds Appeal Dice add it to the Appeal Dice of the Move it was used on.

Voltage

All of the judges start at 1 Voltage for both contest rounds. The Voltage is seen by all performers during both rounds. The judges may have their favorite contest type of moves that they like to see and whenever that type is used increase the judges voltage by 1. If the opposite contest type of move is used, decrease the voltage by 1. Do know that a judge can like multiple different contest types. Or if the contest types are opposites, they just increase the voltage when used. Whenever a Voltage reaches their maximum a powerful boost of appeal is granted to the performer who increased the Voltage.
