Quick rules & info

Cheat Sheet

Challenge Actions

  • Swords: Attack, Riposte
  • Pent: Avoid, Dash, Dodge, Roughhouse
  • Cups: Aid, Command, Pack, Item
  • Wands: Banter, Incant, Recover

Camp Actions

Fellowship, Fletch, Hunt, Patrol, Read, Rest & Recover, Scout, Train, Update Map, Use Item, Use Talent

Tests of Fate

  • Success on 14 or more.
  • If the first card drawn is the correct suit, Great Success.
  • Great Failure on a total of less than 14 after drawing a second card to push fate
  • Favor/Disfavor +/-3

Paths

  • Path of Swords: Aegis, Doom Eye, Heavy Metal Machine, Monster Hunter, Reaver, Two-Handed Focus, War Stories*
  • Path of Pentacles: Acrobat, Ambusher, Con Artist, Fight Dirty, Quick!, Sneak, Up My Sleeve
  • Path of Cups: Alchemy, Beast Master, Bookworm, Chirugeon, Counsel, High Chant, Loremaster
  • Path of Wands: Counter-spells, Dwimmercraft, Gramarye, Magic of the Wastes, Magic of the Weald, Magic of the Weird, Magic of the Welkin

Weapons

  • Axe: If a target is killed, apply same Attack to another foe in range
  • Blade: Deal 2 Wounds on a successful Riposte
  • Flail: Always wins ties (i.e., against foes with shields)
  • Dagger: Deals Piercing damage when a foe is Rooted, Tripped, or Disarmed
  • Hammer & Mace: Deal 2 Wounds when Attack is 2x foe's Initiative
  • Polearm: Play a Riposte as an interrupt
  • Bow: May Aim as an action and add the Aim value to your next Attack
  • Crossbow: Deals Piercing damage, reload with a miscellaneous action
  • Shield: Play any card facedown as a miscellaneous action, replacing Initiative; you win ties in Attack vs Initiative while carrying a shield

Conditions Reference

Stressed

Stressed represents being physically or mentally exhausted.

  • Becoming Stressed is usually the result of environmental effects, such as being covered in sewage, being buried alive in a grave, or wading through an icy river.
  • Wounds do not cause you to be Stressed.
  • You cannot heal any other condition until you are no longer Stressed.

Staggered

Staggered represents being off balance and out of breath.

  • This condition has no immediate negative effects.
  • Staggered is healed automatically during the Camp Phase as long as you’re not Stressed.
  • It is usually the first condition players choose when Wounded. It’s kind of a “free hit.”

Wounded

Your talents can be Wounded. You may have up to two talents Wounded at a time.

  • Wounded talents cannot be used until they are healed.
  • If you already have two talents Wounded, you must choose another condition to mark.

Injured

Injured represents being severely hurt.

  • If you are Injured, the next Wound must give you the Death’s Door condition.
  • During the Camp Phase, you must heal the Injured condition before healing Wounded talents.
  • Because being Injured puts you in danger of death, most players only take this condition if they have no other choice.

Death’s Door

Death’s Door represents being (almost) dead. You are unconscious and helpless.

  • Because of the nature of the Underworld, if nobody moves to heal you within a watch, you will become undead.
