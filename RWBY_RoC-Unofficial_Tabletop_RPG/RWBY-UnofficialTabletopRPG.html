Attributes
STR
Strength
Represents the Character’s ability to
manipulate the world through physical effort.
Skill checks that involve the Character
trying to physically overpower an obstacle
will use STR. Characters with a high STR tend
to be melee focused and have high damage output.
PER
Perception
Represents the senses and the strength of
a character’s Aura. Skill checks that require
a keen eye or the ability to pick up on
hidden clues use PER. Characters with a high
PER can defend themselves against any threat
and are adept at controlling the battlefield.
HP
Health
The amount of physical damage one can withstand
before succumbing to their wounds.
When health reaches 0, the Character is knocked
unconscious and left at the mercy of their enemies.
Health is recovered by 1 at the end of a Scene,
and Health is completely restored a the end of
a Chapter.
/
Endurance + 1
AGI
Agility
Represents the Character’s ability to move
quickly or precisely. Skill checks that require
sudden and rapid movement or accuracy use AGI.
Characters with a high AGI are the most skilled
at ranged combat and are highly mobile.
WIL
Willpower
Represents the Character’s ability to influence
the world with their mind. WIL is used mostly for
Semblance checks as well as conventional influence
Skill checks such as lying or bribing. Characters
with a high WIL have powerful Semblances and are
capable of rolling higher than other characters,
but at a cost.
AUR
Aura
Aura is the passive physical manifestation of the soul.
Every human and Faunas generates an Aura,
and Huntsmen and Huntresses have been trained
in its utilization. Aura is an extra layer of
protection against Damage and is depleted when
attacks cannot be avoided. A Character can also
attempt to influence the world around them with
their Aura in the form of a Semblance.
When Aura reaches 0, the character's Semblance
cannot be used.
Aura completely recharges at the end of a Scene.
/
Perception + 10
END
Endurance
Represents the Character’s stamina and durability.
Skill checks that involve withstanding a hazard or
maintaining effort over a long period of time will
use END. Characters with a high END are amond the
toughest on the field and are well rounded offesively.
DIS
Discipline
Represents the Character’s training and study.
Skill checks that require a clear mind and calm
thoughts, like those to control Dust, will use DIS.
A character with a high DIS is highly versatile and
has numerous Specializations and high Capacity,
making them effective at controlling Elemental Dust.
CAP
Capacity
Capacity is an abstract representation of the total
amount of ammunition a character has access to in a
given moment. Capacity is reduced when a character
uses a Ranged Attack, Dust, or certain combat Action.
All uses of Capacity drain from the same pool.
When Capacity reaches 0, the character cannot perform
any action that requires it.
Capacity is refilled by the character's DIS+1 when they
reload as a Minor Action.

Defenses Defense Checks
Characters who avoid an attack by 5 or more
may make a counter attack as their reaction
instead of the normal bonus for avoiding the
attack. This Counter must be an Attack that
shares an Attribute with the Defense Check.
For exemple: if a characters defends themself
with a Semblance Check, they can Counter Attack
using either WIL or PER, such as an Energy Ranged
Attack, Semblance Attack, or a WIL-based Dust
Attack.
They may also choose to perform any Minor Actionn.
Characters may at any time
choose to do nothing with their reaction.
Parry
Defense Check PER+STR
Preempting the strike and redirecting the
damage elsewhere. Melee attacks and most
physical hazards like falling boulders can be
parried, but Ranged Attacks and incorporeal
hazards like explosions cannot. When a character
successfully parries an attack, they immediately
inflict 3 points of Damage.
Dodge
Defense Check PER+AGI
Moving out of the way as quickly as possible.
Almost all Attacks and hazards can be avoided by
dodging except in special cases such as poison
gas. Successfully dodging a hazard allows the
character to immediately move up to one Range
Increment away or perform any simple Skill Check
that does not require a roll.
Resist
Defense Check PER+END
Reliance on the durability of the body to absorb
any ill effects. Characters can attempt to resist
any hazard regardless of source. If a character
does not succeed at making a Resist Defensive
Check, they may choose to take 1 point of damage
to Health rather than the normal damage to Aura.
Semblance
Defense Check PER+WIL
Semblances can be used to avoid damage, but
which attack and hazards can be avoided are
based on how the Semblance is used. The character
must have at least 1 point of Aura to make
this check.
Before rolling a Defensive Check, characters can
choose to Empower their Semblance at the cost
of 1 Aura.
Empower
Check to empower Semblance defense roll
Temporarily adds 1d10 to the roll at
the cost of 1 aura.
Dust
Defense Check PER+DIS
Dust Phial effects can be used to avoid damage,
but which attack and hazards can be avoided are
based on how the Dust is used.
The Character consumes 1 Capacity to make this check.
Dust Defensive Checks can be capacity boosted
by spending 1 additional Capacity.

Melee Style
The wielding style of your Weapon's melee form.
Aggressive - STR+END
Weapons designed for two-handed use, even if the
character does not so. Includes Buldgeoning and large
weapons like hammers, shields, scythes and claymores but
also unarmed attacks, gauntlets and other worn weapons
Precise - STR+AGI
Small, one-handed weapons. Usually small or thin. Includes
daggers, rapiers, claws and the like.
Elegant - STR+DIS
Any weapon that requires further specialised training.
Straight swords, whips, or staves rely more on careful
training than physical prowess. Dust-ready weapons
usually are Elegant but don't always have to be.

The wielding style of your Weapon's ranged form.
Heavy - AGI+END
Large or unwieldy, requires a great deal of strength.
Heavy machine guns, shotguns, explosive launchers, bows

Accurate - AGI+DIS
Best used when carefully aimed. Pistols and rifles are
typical examples, like most conventional firearms.
Energy - AGI+WIL
Deals damage with bolts of energy or by tapping into
the wielder's Semblance. These bolts are directed with
force of will, bending and writhing toward the target,
but can only hit what the wielder can see.

Alternate Forms
Style
Alternate forms are additional Melee or Ranged styles.
They are granted by a Modification.
By using their weapon in a suboptimal form, such as a sword
rather than a scythe, the wielder trades accuracy for power,
dealing significantly more damage.
When using this alternate style, they recieve a damage
bonus equal to the accuracy penalty compared to their
primary weapon form(that is to say: the difference between
the sums of the attributes used by each style).
This bonus does not carry over between ranged and melee
forms (and is calculated automatically by the sheet).
Alternate attack
Damage+
This number is the amount of bonus damage your alternate form deals. It is equal to the difference between parent and alternate style accuracy bonuses(sum of attributes). (Example: a +9 Aggressive weapon(for STR 5 + END 4) gives a +5 Elegant alternate form(for STR 5 + DIS 0) a bonus of 9-5=4 to alternate form(Elegant) damage.)

Semblance Semblance is the active manifestation of the Character’s soul. Every Huntsman and Huntress has a Semblance that is unique to them.
Semblance checks are always a WIL Check and one other attribute which depends on how the Semblance is being utilized. Semblances last 1 turn, and characters can keep the effect up with a WIL+END Semblance check each subsequent turn or without any Check if they do nothing else during that turn.

Semblances can also be used to make attacks up to Close
Range, dealing Damage equal to the character's WIL.

One can Empower their Semblance after they roll the dice, adding 1d10 to that roll at the cost of 1 Aura. A button will appear in the chat after rolling, click it to Empower your Semblance.

Crystal Making a melee attack with equiped Dust costs
1 Capacity and produces the effect of the Dust
cristal for 1 turn.
Status Effects caused by Dust last 1 turn and are
wiped away and replaced if any new Status Effect

round for 1 turn.

Phials
Using a Dust Phial is a Minor Action and consumes 2 Capacity.
Describe what aspect of the Dust’s element you want to manipulate
and make a Dust Check.

One can also Attack up to Close Range with a Phial, using a Major Action.
This costs 2 Capacity and deals Damage equal to the character's DIS.

Before Attacking with a Dust Phial, a character can consume an additional
Capacity to apply the Dust Crystal or Round Effect to the attack if
successful.
