Duelist
Equipment: Southern duelists traditionally carry a rapier. Other factions may have a certain melee weapon traditional for dueling or dueling pistol.
Rank: Duelists rank up by defeating significant opponents and performing showy and often impressive feats of piloting and skill.
Momentum: /
Special Rules
R-1 : Select 1 Special Move, this Special Move may be performed any number of times during an activation. The Duelist may change the selected move each time they rank up or as an intermission action.
R-2 : Attempts to prevent the Duelist from leaving an enemy’s melee range automatically fail.
R-3 : The Duelist may increase the cost of Special Moves to reduce the penalty imposed for taking multiple actions in a single activation at a rate of 1 Momentum per 1d6 of penalty. This may be done multiple times and on multiple Special Moves during the same Activation
R-4 : The Duelist may spend 1 Momentum during their Activation to allow 1 friendly actor to reroll any single skill test. The reroll expires at the end of the scene if it is not used
R-5 : The Duelist can make a single melee attack as a free action during their Activation
R-6 : When resisting melee attacks, instead of simply resisting, the Duelist may declare it a Riposte with one of their melee weapons. If the Duelist wins the roll, inflict damage as if it was an attack using the Duelist’s MoS. The original aggressor still wins ties
Display Fixer
Equipment: The Fixer may select up to 2 total burden of items to count as archetype equipment.
Rank: Fixers rank up for any actions that advance the story or help the team’s mission succeed
Luck: / Special Rules
R-1 : At the start of each session, the Fixer gains a pool of Luck dice they can add to any skill test. This is determined before rolling. Any dice added to a roll are lost. They may add all dice at once, or one at a time. Either way, unspent dice are lost and the pool refills each session
R-2 : The Fixer may choose to mark one XP on a skill each session whether they used the skill or not. This takes the place of the usual XP they would be allowed to gain for that skill.
R-3 : Once per mission, when selecting a vehicle or a piece of equipment needed for that task, the character can select a piece that is one priority level higher than normal. At Director discretion, this can be used to get PL 1 equipment from a faction the character has priority level 0 with
R-4 : The Fixer gains an additional intermission action that can be used for one of the following actions: Collect Intel, Develop Contacts, Acquire, Get into Trouble, or Special. This action can only take effect if the Director determines there is enough time for the action to take place.
R-5 : Once per session, when a non-attack skill test fails by MoF no greater than 2, the Fixer can take a -1d6 Shell Shock damage result to have the roll count as an MoS: 0 instead. This action counts as a reroll for the purposes of rerolls
R-6 : Once per session, the Fixer can add a specific fact to the results of one roll. They could use this to specify an interesting detail about an attack, protect themselves from a specific consequence, or turn the tables unexpectedly by having information that can be used to blackmail an antagonist in their gear’s databanks. This could be used for something as simple as saying the Fixer has a piece of gear they forgot to bring, to something more complex like “even though I failed to talk my way out of getting arrested, my scenario was sympathetic enough that one of the police will contact my friends”. The fact has to fit the story and the Director is allowed veto over absurd or destructive requests, but otherwise it is the Fixer’s choice.

Display Pilot
Equipment: Pilot Suit.
Rank: Pilots rank up for performing significant acts of skill or valor.
Special Rules
R-1 : The Pilot must select an actor type from the following list; Gear, Strider, Vehicle, Aircraft or Drone. This is their specialization. Pilot archetype effects only apply when the Pilot is using that type of vehicle unless otherwise stated. The Pilot can perform a Patch action once per scene on the vehicle they are piloting. This Patch only ever patches 1d6 worth of penalty of the Pilot’s choice and is a free action.
R-2 : The first Reload or Ram actions during the Pilot’s activation is a free action.
R-3 : The Pilot may select either of two effects whenever their vehicle recieves maintenance, only one is active at a time. Increase the maximum and optimal range of all ranged weapons by 10m (1in) of the vehicle the pilot is piloting for every full 120m (12in) of maximum range, or increase the MR of the vehicle the Pilot is piloting by 1. Increase this by an additional 1 for every full 8 of MR.
R-4 : The pilot can use Focus as a free action and does not spend an action point to do so
R-5 : Once per Activation when attacking a target the Pilot can make an additional attack against the same target as a free action.
R-6 : The Pilot may select either of the two following effects. The Pilot gains 1 action point while piloting their chosen vehicle type, or the Pilot can select 1 additional actor type for their vehicle specialization. All Pilot rank effects apply when using either of the selected actor types.
